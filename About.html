Who am I?

I'm a Chinese-Canadian communications professional and freelance creative. I strive to never lose my sense of wonder, and stay adventurous and optimistic in all things.

I'm a proud graduate of Human Ecology (BSc, University of Alberta) and Professional Communication (MA, Royal Roads University), bringing over a decade of healthcare comms to my vocal work. I've worked across the life span with children and older adults, and found my interviewing and counseling skills lending well to working in a vocal booth. My first VO work began this way, with in-house promos, medical explainers, and social stories for children.

My first voiceover class was in 2019, and I've been hooked ever since! Over the years I've played independent detectives, concerned parents, vengeful goddesses, sultry singers, and more.  Read my VO resume here. 

If you're exploring other BIPOC/PGM talent for your projects, I invite you to check out Mixed Asian Media (MAM) and the PGM VO List. 

Outside the booth, I've been and done a lot of things over the years. I met my husband playing video games together; earned a private pilot's license when I was 19 and flew a Spitfire at 21 (boats, motorcycles and cars, small planes? I'm your getaway driver); was a panhellenic sorority president in my undergrad years; co-authored a journal publication in human ecology in my grad years; travelled and embraced a digital nomad life for work, for professional development, for love, from Bloemfontein to â€‹Bromstadvegen.

I love exploring new worlds and look forward to yours.
